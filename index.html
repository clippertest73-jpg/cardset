The Universe

The year is 2387. Humanity has colonised 14 star systems. Something in the dark has been listening. And now it is answering.

"For 300 years, the astronomers of the Sovereign Reach catalogued recurring anomalous signals from beyond the Outer Veil. They called them noise. They were catastrophically wrong."
— From the Series 1 Collector's Insert
Human Empire · Dominant Force
The Sovereign Reach

A cold, orderly civilisation spanning 14 star systems, built on ruthless efficiency and enforced silence. The Reach has suppressed all records of the Void signals for three centuries. Admirals disappear. Scientists are reassigned. The official position: there is nothing out there. The unofficial position: classified at the highest level.

Cards in set: 38 · Types: Characters, Vessels, Classified Documents
Signature ships: Dreadnought-class, Imperial Courier
Card style: Cold blues, bureaucratic precision
Colony Coalition · Insurgent Power
The Varyn Concord

A loose coalition of breakaway colonies and alien client-states living at the margins of Reach territory. They've been receiving the signals too — and unlike the Reach, they haven't been classifying them. They've been decoding them. What they've found has split the Concord in two: those who want to respond, and those who want to run.

Cards in set: 32 · Types: Characters, Ships, Decoded Transmissions
Signature ships: Salvager-class, Relay Drifter
Card style: Warm ochres, worn edges, hand-annotated
Ancient Alien Civilisation · Fanatical
The Ossara Hegemony

They ruled the Outer Veil before humanity arrived. Their empire was ancient when Earth's first cities were being built. They know what lives beyond the Veil — they encountered it ten thousand years ago — and their civilisation has been shaped by that encounter ever since. Their hull designs bear symbols no human linguist can parse. They are not afraid. That is the most frightening thing about them.

Cards in set: 34 · Types: Characters, Warships, Sacred Relics
Signature ships: Veilcutter, Temple Carrier
Card style: Deep reds, gold sigils, alien geometry
Unknown Origin · Cosmic Scale
The Void

Not a faction. Not a species. Not a force in any political sense. The Void entities are older than any of the star systems the three empires fight over. They do not communicate in language. They communicate in mathematics — the signals — and every civilisation that has received them has been fundamentally changed by the act of understanding them. They are never fully depicted on any card. Only glimpsed. Only implied.

Cards in set: 16 · Types: Phenomena, Signal Fragments, Holographic Variants
Card style: Abstract, geometric, deeply unsettling. Holographic foil only.

The Horror Layer

The cosmic horror in Signals from the Void is never explicit. It operates at the edges.

Rule 01

The Void entities are never fully depicted on any card. Only glimpsed, implied, or reflected in the faces of those who have seen them. The horror is in the reaction, not the subject.

Rule 02

Holographic cards show mathematical patterns — the signals themselves. Collectors report that looking at them for too long feels wrong. The geometry doesn't resolve the way geometry should.

Rule 03

Certain Common cards have intentional printing "errors" — corrupted borders, inverted text fragments — hinting at Void influence seeping into the set itself. Finding them is part of the game.

Rule 04

Ultra Rare cards depict the moment a character receives a signal — the instant of revelation, not the aftermath. The expression on their face is the only clue as to what they saw.

About Ætherwave

A small-batch collectible card label dedicated to the golden age of science fiction.

The Label

Ætherwave Collectibles was founded with one simple obsession: the feeling of opening a science fiction paperback from the 1950s and seeing that cover illustration for the first time. The saturated colours. The impossible spacecraft. The alien skies. The sense that the universe was enormous and terrifying and magnificent.

We make trading cards that try to bottle that feeling. Each card is designed as an object in its own right — something worth looking at, worth holding, worth keeping. The linen texture finish, the hand-painted aesthetic, the considered rarity system — these aren't features. They're the whole point.

The Craft

Every illustration in the Signals from the Void set was created with a brief: make it look like it was painted in 1963. Bold outlines for Common cards. Rich gouache for Uncommon. Full oil painting treatment for Rares. And for the Holographic variants — something that shouldn't be possible to paint at all.

Cards are printed on 350gsm premium stock with a linen texture finish. Standard trading card dimensions: 2.5 × 3.5 inches. Rated 8+ for content.
